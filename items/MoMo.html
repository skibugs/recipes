MoMo


Description

Momo is a popular Tibetan and Nepali dumpling made with a thin flour dough filled with a mixture of minced meat or vegetables, garlic, ginger, onions, and spices. The dough is rolled into small circles, stuffed with the filling, and then folded into a pleated shape. These dumplings are steamed, fried, or boiled until fully cooked. They are typically served with spicy tomato chutney or sesame dipping sauce. Momos are a delicious and comforting snack enjoyed across South Asia.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup water (adjust as needed)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon oil (optional)
  • 1 cup minced chicken or mixed vegetables (cabbage, carrots, mushrooms)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ teaspoon soy sauce (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro (optional)
  • 2 tomatoes, boiled and blended
  • 2 dried red chilies, soaked
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • ½ teaspoon sesame seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon oil

Steps:

  • Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, salt, and water. Knead until smooth and cover it for 30 minutes.
  • Make the Filling: In a separate bowl, mix minced chicken or vegetables, chopped onion, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, black pepper, salt, cilantro, and oil.
  • Roll the Dough: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into thin circles of about 3 inches in diameter.
  • Fill the Momos: Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each circle. Fold and pleat the edges to seal the momo properly.
  • Steam the Momos: Place the prepared momos in a greased steamer and steam for 10-15 minutes until fully cooked.
  • Prepare the Dipping Sauce: Blend boiled tomatoes, soaked red chilies, garlic, sesame seeds, salt, and oil into a smooth sauce.
  • Serve and Enjoy: Serve the hot momos with the spicy dipping sauce and enjoy!
